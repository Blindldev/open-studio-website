Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our studio

Membership

How do I become a member?

To become a member, please fill out our application form. After reviewing your application, we'll contact you to schedule a studio tour and discuss membership details.

When is the next studio tour?

If you'd like a tour of our studio, please email us to schedule a visit. This is a great opportunity to see our space and learn more about our membership program.

What's included in the membership?

Membership includes 24/7 studio access, 25 lbs of clay per month, and unlimited glazing and firing of your work.

Can I bring guests?

Guests are not allowed in the open studio space. However, members' friends and family can receive discounted access to our main studio classes and activities.

Studio Usage

What are the studio hours?

The studio is open 24/7 except for Mondays 9pm to Tuesdays 9am (12 hours weekly) when it is closed for cleaning, moving pieces for firing, and allowing the floor to properly dry after mopping. This is also when all bisque firing takes place in the open studio space.

What equipment is available?

We have 7 pottery wheels, a handbuilding table, electric kilns, and various tools and equipment for ceramic work.

Do you offer classes?

No, we are a studio space for independent work only. We do not offer classes in our space. However, classes are available in the main studio space at The Pottery Loop, where you can find a variety of pottery classes and workshops.

Materials & Firing

What materials are provided?

Members receive 25 pounds of clay per month. Additional clay can be purchased at $2 per pound in 25-pound increments. Simply message us for more clay.

How often do you fire the kilns?

We typically fire the kilns every week. Check our firing updates page for the schedule.

Can I bring my own clay?

No, we only allow the use of studio-provided clay to ensure compatibility with our kilns and glazes. This also ensures resources are used efficiently and fairly.

How does the firing process work?

All pieces must be bisque fired in the open studio space before they can be glazed. Glazing will take place in the main studio where the main glazed pieces kiln is located. Important notes about firing:

  • Each member is responsible for tracking how long ago they made each piece
  • Pieces must dry for AT LEAST 10 days before bisque firing (ideally 14 days for thicker pieces)
  • Initial all pieces and add notes or take pictures to track when they were made
  • If a piece explodes during bisque firing, that is the member's responsibility
  • Any questions about the firing process should be directed to Mike
