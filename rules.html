Studio Rules

Guidelines to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for all members

Studio Rules

  • Clean up after yourself - this includes wheels, tables, and tools
  • Respect other artists' space and work
  • No food or drink near the wheels
  • Only allocated clay allowed NO OUTSIDE CLAY
  • Label all your work clearly
  • Follow proper safety procedures
  • All members are responsible for reclaiming their own clay (this means 2 separate bags and wedging your own clay)

Note: While the 24-hour space doesn't allow guests, members can receive discounts for bringing friends to the regular studio space!

Equipment Usage

  • Wheels must be cleaned after each use
  • Tools must be returned to their designated places
  • Report any broken or malfunctioning equipment
  • Follow proper procedures for kiln loading and unloading
  • No unauthorized equipment modifications

Safety Protocols

  • Wear appropriate clothing and closed-toe shoes
  • Follow all posted safety guidelines
  • Keep exits clear at all times
  • Know the location of first aid kits and fire extinguishers

Membership Requirements

  • Valid membership code must be entered upon entry and you must bring your locker key (no personal lock needed)
  • Follow all posted studio hours
  • Complete required safety training before using equipment
  • Keep contact information up to date

Guest Policy

  • Members are welcome to bring guests to the main studio space.
  • Guests must be accompanied by a member at all times.
  • The Independent Study Room is reserved exclusively for members to prioritize their work and maintain a focused environment.
  • Guests are not permitted to use studio equipment or materials.

Studio Hours

  • The studio is open 24/7 except for Wednesdays 9pm to Thursdays 9am
  • This 12-hour weekly closure is for:
    • Cleaning and maintenance
    • Moving pieces for firing
    • Allowing the floor to properly dry after mopping
    • Conducting all bisque firing in the open studio space
  • No exceptions to the closure time - plan your work accordingly
  • All members must note their planned studio time in the shared calendar to help manage space and avoid congestion during busy periods

Firing Procedures

  • All pieces must be bisque fired in the open studio space before glazing
  • Glazing takes place during normal studio hours
  • Glazed pieces will be fired upstairs
  • Members are responsible for:
    • Tracking how long ago each piece was made
    • Initialing all pieces
    • Adding notes or taking pictures to track creation dates
    • Ensuring pieces dry for AT LEAST 14 days before bisque firing (ideally 18 days for thicker pieces)
  • Members assume responsibility if pieces explode during bisque firing
  • Direct any questions about the firing process to Mike
