Candle Making Rules & Guidelines

Personal Use

The candle making equipment is primarily intended for personal use. If you're making candles for yourself, friends, or family, you're welcome to use the studio's wax and supplies at no additional cost!

Selling Candles

If you plan to sell candles and make a large quantity, that's absolutely fine! However, we ask that you bring your own wax for commercial production. This helps us keep supplies available for everyone.

Wax Melter Safety

  • Warm-up Time: The wax melter needs to run for approximately 20 minutes before it's ready to pour. Please be patient and wait for it to reach the proper temperature.
  • When You're Done: Always turn off and unplug the wax melter when you're finished. Safety first!
  • Clean Up: Please clean up any spilled wax and return all supplies to their designated areas.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Ceramic Candles

1

Choose Your Ceramic Piece

Select a bisque-fired ceramic piece you've made (or one that's been fired). Make sure it's clean and free of any glaze that might interfere with the candle.

2

Select Your Wick

Choose an appropriately sized wick for your vessel. The wick should be tall enough to extend above the wax once poured, plus about 1 inch extra.

3

Secure the Wick

Use a wick sticker or hot glue to secure the wick to the bottom center of your ceramic vessel. Keep it straight using a wick holder or by tying it to a pencil across the top.

4

Prepare the Wax Melter

Turn on the wax melter and allow it to warm up for about 20 minutes. This ensures the wax will melt evenly and reach the proper pouring temperature.

5

Melt Your Wax

Measure the wax you need (account for shrinkage - you'll need slightly more than your vessel's volume). Add it to the melter and wait until it's fully melted and reaches around 185¬∞F.

6

Add Fragrance (Optional)

Once the wax is melted, add your chosen fragrance oil (typically 6-10% of wax weight). Stir gently and let cool slightly to 135-145¬∞F before pouring.

7

Pour the Wax

Carefully pour the wax into your ceramic vessel, leaving about ¬º inch from the top. Pour slowly to avoid air bubbles and keep the wick centered.

8

Let It Set

Allow the candle to cool and set completely (this can take several hours). As it cools, you may notice a slight depression around the wick - this is normal!

9

Top Off (If Needed)

After the candle has set, if there's a significant depression, you can melt a bit more wax and top it off. Trim the wick to about ¬º inch before lighting.

10

Clean Up

Turn off and unplug the wax melter. Clean any spilled wax and return all supplies to their proper places. Enjoy your beautiful handmade candle!

Pro Tips

  • Test your ceramic piece for water tightness before making a candle
  • Wider vessels may need multiple wicks for even burning
  • Let candles cure for 24-48 hours before lighting for best results
  • Burn candles for at least 2-3 hours on first use to create a full melt pool
  • Never leave burning candles unattended
